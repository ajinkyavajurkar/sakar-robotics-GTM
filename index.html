Policy tailwinds

Swachh Bharat Mission completed 10 years in 2024 with ₹12,192 crore budget allocation. Govt mandates hygiene compliance across airports, hospitals, and public infrastructure, creating institutional demand for auditable cleaning outcomes. Source: IBEF

FM sector growth

India's integrated FM market grows at 10.2% CAGR from $5.3B (2025) to $8.6B by 2030. Top FMCs are actively differentiating through tech to win and retain large enterprise contracts. Source: Mordor Intelligence

Commercial infra boom

New airports (Navi Mumbai, Jewar, Bhogapuram), pharma SEZs, and hospital construction under Ayushman Bharat are creating large-footprint facilities that need consistent, scalable, compliance-grade cleaning. The commercial segment is growing at 33.9% CAGR within the cleaning robot market. Source: DBMR

The strategic window

India's commercial cleaning robot market is the fastest growing in APAC but still dominated by residential units. The B2B commercial segment is early. FMCs are beginning to explore robotics, but no Indian player has yet built a scalable, service-backed commercial cleaning robot business. Sakar enters while distribution channels are unoccupied and reference accounts are scarce.

Slide 07: Revenue Model

Three revenue
streams. One relationship.

Hardware is the entry point. Consumables and service contracts are where Sakar builds a defensible, recurring business.

Stream 01

Hardware sales

Primary revenue driver. Each unit at ₹15L–30L generates ₹5L–12L gross margin depending on volume and model mix. Volume discounts for fleet purchases of 5+ units incentivize FMC adoption at scale.

~60% of revenue (Year 1)
Stream 02

Consumables

Brushes, scrubbing pads, filters, and cleaning solution, recurring quarterly or monthly orders per robot. At ₹8,000–15,000/month per unit, a fleet of 20 robots generates ₹2L–3L/month in consumables alone.

~25% of revenue (Year 2+)
Stream 03

AMC / Service contracts

Annual Maintenance Contracts at 8–12% of hardware value. A ₹20L robot generates ₹1.6L–2.4L/year in AMC revenue. Stackable, predictable, and the key to long-term customer lock-in.

~15% of revenue (Year 2+)

Client context

Serum Institute of India, Pune

World's largest vaccine manufacturer by volume. Operates in highly regulated, sterile environments where cleanliness is not optional. It's a compliance requirement tied to WHO and GMP certifications.

This is the hardest possible environment to deploy a cleaning robot in India. If it works here, it works everywhere.

The challenge they faced

Maintaining consistent cleaning standards across large manufacturing and warehouse floors with manual labor: managing absenteeism, ensuring cleaning logs for audits, and reducing contamination risk.

How to use this in sales

Lead every hospital, pharma, and high-compliance facility conversation with this reference. Offer to connect prospects directly with the Serum FM team. A warm reference in this sector closes faster than any pitch deck.

Serum is the reference. Lead with regulatory compliance and audit trail as the primary value. Price is secondary.

Slide 10: Network & Leverage

Shortcuts that
others don't see.

India's B2B market runs on relationships and references. Don't just go direct. Build a network that sends deals to Sakar.

Channels to activate

Industry associations

Indian Facility Management Council (IFMC), CII, and FICCI have facility management working groups. Present at one event as a thought leader on "autonomous cleaning in India." One talk generates 10 warm leads.

Trade shows

CleanIndia 2025 (Mumbai), BuildTech Expo, and Hospital Management Asia are where FM decision-makers gather. A live demo of the robot on the floor beats a hundred cold calls.

Cleaning equipment distributors

India has a network of industrial cleaning equipment dealers (Eureka Forbes B2B division, regional Kärcher distributors). Some are looking for alternatives. A dealer who already calls on FMCs every month can carry Sakar's robots into conversations Sakar can't reach alone.

Real estate / project consultants

Large real estate developers (Embassy, DLF, Godrej Properties) often specify FM requirements during construction. Getting Sakar into a green-field specification means the FMC that wins the contract inherits Sakar as the robot vendor.

What to leverage now

1
Serum Institute reference

Use as an introduction in every pharma and hospital conversation. Ask Serum for a written testimonial and the FM team's willingness to take reference calls.

2
Chinese manufacturer relationship

The manufacturer likely has references from other APAC deployments: Singapore, Indonesia, Middle East. Use global case studies to validate quality before India references exist.

3
LinkedIn outreach to FM heads

FMC decision-makers are active on LinkedIn. A short video of the robot cleaning a large floor area, with the Serum Institute context, is the most shareable content Sakar can produce.

4
FMC-as-partner model

Offer UDS, Quess, or SIS a co-sell arrangement: they bring the client, Sakar supplies and services the robot, they earn a referral margin. Aligns incentives without requiring Sakar to build a large direct sales team immediately.
